About me

Hi, I'm Zack! I'm currently in my final year of Software and Biomedical Engineering at McMaster University. I often get asked 'why software and biomedical?' Before I enetered tech, I was set on medical school with the intent of making positive change in peoples lives. I've always been motived by the impact of my work and thought that healthcare was the only field where I could get that gratification. After doing my first software project I quickly realized how powerful tech is and infinite opportunities to make change. Since then, I've been exploring different domains of software engineering and building things that are meaningful to me. Software engineering continues to challenge me and keeps me curious to learn more and the impact of the work I do is what keeps me motivated.

During my journey through tech, I've had the opportunity to work on a variety of different projects in disciplines ranging from Machine Learning to Biotechnology. Along the way, I've had hands-on experience with numerous programming languages such as C++, Java, and Python to new a few. From past projects, work experience, and coursework, I've learned about mobile development, cloud computing, data structures and algorithms, software engineering practices, and much more. In this online portfolio, I've recounted some of my past work experiences and notable projects that I'm proud of.

On days when I decide I've had enough of staring into a monitor, I enjoy going out for a hike or at least doing something active. Recently, I've picked up playing badminton again after a couple a couple year hiatus. I'm always on the lookout for new things to learn and coffee shops to try!

Work

On the mobile back-end team, I built features for customers Canada-wide. Within the mobile message center service, I architected two endpoints, one to collect user feedback on message and another to enable message expiry. These new endpoints allow Capital One Canada to push personalized messages to customers based on the the interactions with the messages and ultimately improves user experience within the app. There was a lot of collaboration with Product and Front-end regarding the design descisions to ensure code maintainability. I got to work with Java, Spring, AWS, Ruby, Docker, Jenkins and more throughout the software development lifecycle.

At this biotech startup, I got to experience a lot of different things. From prototype automation on one day to data analysis on the next, the unpredictable schedule was a refreshing change of pace from a typical 9-5. I worked closely with the assay team to develop a custom Python library designed to automate parts of the workflow. I also performed data analysis to compare improvements between different vision pipelines using the Python libraries pandas, numPy and matplotlib. I really enjoyed the days when I would go into the lab and run some experiments; it was super cool seeing the tangible outcomes of the code I wrote.

At Coveo, I worked in the machine learning team, under the user profiling sub-team. The main project I was working on during the term was Profile Dynamic Configuration. The goal was to provide clients the ability to set configurations for their individual models without requiring an application restart, ultimately saving costs for Coveo and allowing for quicker deployments and more flexibility. This was my first encounter with Scala; however, I was already familiar with Java so it wasn't difficult to pick up. Some other technologies I used were Spark, Spring, and AWS (DynamoDB, S3, EC2, EMR).

As a TA, I lead weekly tutorials and labs. The most enjoyable part of this experience was getting to interact with students and help them understand the material.
